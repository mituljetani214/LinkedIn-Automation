## Munim 2.1: Redesigning Cloud Accounting For India's CAs And MSMEs

### Summary

Munim is a cloud accounting and billing SaaS platform for Indian Chartered Accountants and MSME business owners. Mitul led the UX/UI redesign for Munim 2.1 across architecture, interaction design, and design systems.

### Role

Sole UX/UI owner within a 3-person team.

### Problem

Munim's earlier experience carried too many fields in dense, single-scroll flows. CA users were used to fast, keyboard-driven tools like Tally, but the cloud interface felt unstable and slower because fields appeared dynamically and key tasks required too much navigation.

### Research

- Interviews with CAs and non-technical MSME owners
- Usage analytics and heatmaps
- Competitive benchmarking against Tally, Zoho Books, and Vyapar
- Invoice creation timing benchmark

### Key Design Decisions

- Added a global quick-add bar for creating records from anywhere.
- Created a favorites/shortcuts bar for repeated daily tasks.
- Expanded the dashboard to show business health signals such as stock, sales, revenue, and restocking needs.
- Built keyboard-first workflows with visible shortcuts.
- Standardized the product through a unified Figma design system.

### Impact

- Reduced invoice creation time from approximately 1 minute 38 seconds to under 30 seconds.
- Contributed significant UX and product design work during Munim's growth from approximately 12,000 to 100,000+ active customers.
- Supported launch of Munim 2.1 on web, followed by native Android and iOS apps.

### Content Angles

- "Good UX starts with a stopwatch, not a screen."
- "How we reduced invoice creation from 1:38 to under 30 seconds."
- "Why accounting UX needs keyboard-first design."
- "Designing cloud accounting for users trained by Tally."

## Group8A: Rebuilding A 100-Client Affiliate Dashboard Around Daily Workflows

### Summary

Group8A is a confidential marketing agency SaaS dashboard used to manage affiliate marketing operations for 100+ clients. Mitul led the UX redesign of a 30+ page web app dashboard and introduced a structured Figma design system.

### Role

UX lead, working directly with the client's Head of Design and one junior designer at Identixweb.

### Problem

The internal dashboard had been in production for over four years and no longer matched how employees worked. Static tables showed the same fields to every role, reporting was disconnected from daily workflow, and high-volume lead tracking was difficult.

### Research

- Contextual inquiry with daily users
- Direct meetings with the client team
- Review of analytics and client reports
- Usability testing across multiple design variants

### Key Design Decisions

- Replaced static tables with role-oriented, filterable table views.
- Reconnected reporting to daily workflow outcomes.
- Introduced a calendar view with lead status indicators.
- Built a real Figma design system for a 30+ page product.
- Ran a visual design pass with variant testing before final direction.

### Impact

- Made daily work roughly 2x faster than before.
- Improved module clarity for internal users.
- Shipped a redesigned 30+ page web SaaS dashboard across admin and sub-account experiences.
- Improved design-to-development consistency through a proper Figma design system.

- "Why role-based dashboards beat one-size-fits-all tables."
- "How to redesign a tool people have used every day for four years."
- "Design systems matter most when a product has 30+ pages."
