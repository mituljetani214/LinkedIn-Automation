# Writing Style

Last updated: 2026-07-23

## Desired Style

- Clear
- Practical
- Simple
- Minimal
- Human
- Evidence-based
- Product-thinking led
- Useful to UI/UX designers, founders, recruiters, and product teams
- Confident without sounding inflated

## Voice Principles

- Explain design through simple, real-world examples.
- Make the problem clear before presenting the solution.
- Connect design decisions to user value and business impact.
- Use AI as a practical workflow advantage, not hype.
- Prefer clear language over clever language.
- Keep personal stories short and useful.

## Approved Phrases

- Design should solve real business problems.
- AI should augment humans, not replace human judgment.
- Human + AI is stronger than either alone.
- Every experience must remain human-centric.
- Never jump directly into design.
- Research first, design second.
- Understand the problem before designing the solution.
- Design is not only about screens; it is about decisions.
- Designing systems, not just screens.
- Simplifying complexity through product thinking.
- Turning user insights into measurable outcomes.
- Building scalable experiences for growing products.
- Good UX starts before Figma.
- A beautiful interface is not enough if the workflow is broken.
- Design should connect user needs, business goals, and technical constraints.

## Avoid

- Generic motivational content
- Fake authority
- Overused hooks
- Unsupported claims
- Trend-only design advice
- AI replacement hype
- Empty thought leadership
- Overly academic UX language
- Claims like "I alone caused this growth"
- "Pixel-perfect" as the main value
- "Beautiful UI" without business or user context

## Signature Themes

- AI-native Product Design
- Human-Centered AI
- SaaS UX
- Enterprise UX
- Product thinking
- UX strategy
- Design systems
- Conversion improvement
- Workflow simplification
- AI-assisted design workflows
- Portfolio storytelling
